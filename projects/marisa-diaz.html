The Guest

Artist Statement

In my animated 3D short, I explore the theme of fear particularly the fear of the "other" and the unknown and its unsettling effect on our sense of security. By transforming the home, a place traditionally associated with safety, into a landscape of anxiety and danger, I reveal how our perceptions of refuge can become distorted by internal and external fears. Through blender and mocap I manipulate digital environments to blur the boundaries between reality and illusion, amplifying the psychological tension of encountering the unfamiliar. This work aims to provoke reflection on how our anxieties can erode our sense of safety and reshape our most intimate spaces into sources of unease.

Marisa Diaz

Bio Marisa Diaz is an Oakland-based artist and student at San Jose State University. Who experiment with various art mediums, both traditional and digital, and have created diverse work. Pursuing a career in digital media art with a focus on video game art and animation at San Jose State. Already published a game in the Google Play store and contributed concept art to triple-A games. Aspiring to create virtual 3D cinematics with compelling narratives that reach a broad audience in the fear of the unknown. Her tools include Adobe Photoshop, Premiere Pro, Illustrator, After Effects, Unity, Unreal 5, Maya, Blender, and Rokoko Mocap. Her ultimate goal is to inspire others to pursue creative endeavors and grow as an multimedia artist.
